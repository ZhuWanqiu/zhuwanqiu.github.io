Periodic Post #4

Shift of the Plan

To be honest, I am still struggling to interview people around the main campus and still had no big process.

I guess people sometimes are too busy to share their lives or thoughts with strangers in real life, but they do love to share online, on Instagram, Snapchat, Facebook, What’s app, Telegram, Wechat, Twitter, Tumblr, Reddit, etc.

So instead of interview, this week I put my focus on communication online and the communication between Chinese people and the local here.

We have learned English for over twelv years and then go abroad for experiencing more. We learned English in various methods - from class, from TV series, from movies, from books, from real-time communication. We spent a lot of time on vocabulary memorizing, grammar understanding, and the time we spent is too much that we somehow forget the purpose of learning a new Language - for mutual communication and understanding.

Back to the time I was in Taiwan and Finland, I met some foreign friends who are interested in learning Mandarin and struggled to find materials for them to learn basic Chinese. They want to know more about Chinese culture and seek opportunity to visit China or even start their business with Chinese manufacturer. What they had was only the old-schooled Mandarin learning Guide book from which what is taught are far away from casual communication. I cannot find useful materials for them so I taught them Mandarin face to face with simple words I assumed. It did not work on all of my friends at that time. Some of them did not have enough knowledge base to understand what I taught, while some of them made incredible progress and asked me to talk to them in Mandarin all the time (but I speak Mandarin too fast so I said no). This phenomenon makes me think a lot.

Same, in New York, I am always astonished by the people who try to say Chinese to me. They shout “Ni Hao”(你好，hello in Chinese) to me and “Guo Yi Xia” (过一下，excuse me in Chinese). Not only the passersby, more and more schools and communities offer Mandarin class and bilingual immersive environment for children to learn Mandarin systematically. And even USNEWS suggests that teaching Mandarin Chinese should be a priority for American students for it can provide educational and economical success for the society.

The Plan so Far

This Wednesday I had a meeting with a friend who teaches Mandarin class as teaching assistant in Columbia University. Instead of classroom drill and kill, we all think language learning should be more flexible and casual. In curriculum design, a class should be divided into three parts: I do, you do and we do. “I do” means teacher’s tutorial, which should be as simple and clear as possible. “You do” means student’s one-sided practice with teacher and will get teacher’s direct feedback. “We do” means student’s practice with other students through collaboration. We find that the both offline and online tutorials all focus on “I do” and “you do” but there are not so much space for learners to practice “we do” with proper materials. In order to provide more opportunities for students to practice “you do” and “we do”, we decided to build an online platform for Mandarin learners to look for professional tutor and practice with other learner in interesting activities.

The functionality of the platform includes but not limits in:

  • Tutorials: Learners can join in the online courses designed by qualified TCSOL teacher and receive immediate feedback.
  • Forum: If they have any question, they can post their question on forum waiting for other learners’ solution. It’s also a playground for language learner to do activities all together.
  • Self-learning: Learners can find online tutorials videos and learn whatever they want and whenever they want in their own pace.
  • Friends: Learners can be friends with other learners and learn all the stuff together. They can also be friends with Mandarin Chinese tutors and have free talk.
  • Open to both learners and TCSOL: It’s a open platform so not only can learners find learning resources, TSCOL tutor can get teaching experience (maybe wages if we launch the project profitable) from this platform.

The platform is still in process…

Week-by-week Timeline

Because the project is due by Dec. 15th and we have a long way to go, so we need speed up.

  • present~11/5: interview with Mandarin learners to investigate the user need and simpply the functionality.
  • present~11/10: homepage design and find more TSCOL tutors in TC.
  • 11/10~11/17: build the forum and upload the tutor information online.
  • 11/17~11/24: invite students from Mandarin class to use and improve functionality.
  • 11/24~12/1: record tutorial videos and upload them online.
  • 12/1~12/8: build database for students to memorize their process and make friends.
  • 12/15: debug;

That’s it. The things we are going to do in the rest of the time are on the timeline but it does not mean we will exactly follow the timeline. For example, finding Mandarin learners and investigate user need is what we will enact all the time and debug is what we are about to do after designing a page.

Periodic Post #3

Explore

I started my interview this Wednesday. I prepared my carmera and set tripot in Morningside campus, but what embraced me was now the smile of people. So sad that students were too busy for midterm and group meeting and no one had time for my interview question.

The busy, never-stopping crowd made me reflect on my plan that it is not highly plausible to make successful interview with restless passersby.

Instead, I made some time laspe for Columbia Morningside Campus this week.

Shift of My Plan

The unsuccessful beginning made me think more about the feasibility of the project.

On-street interview may be not that suitable for story sharing, but what about a platform that people could simply upload their videos and create interaction or relationships based on their video or whatever they upload?

We live in a fragmentational world and our focus can no longer last more than 7s, so place, time, and what you are doing in that place and time matter.

Some people want to look as pretty as possible in their videos; while some want to emphasize on the amazing, mindblowing scenes; some just want to focus on the content of the videos. In this multi-demand world, can we simplify what they need in general?

The intention of my finding a way for people sharing their moments or stories is to reframe deeper relation and understanding between people from different backgrounds or races.

But do they, the users in general, really have a demand to understand each other, in this world?

I believe they do. And I believe that’s the reason people come to New York City, or other new places, from all over the world. They want to experience more and understand more about the thing they never experience before and the people they don’t know before.

Future Plan

In the following week I will keep building homepage for this project and start to think more about platform on which people sharing their moment with each other. The conceptual model of how user interacter each other will be figured out.

In the meanwhile, I will reach out to look for more stories and keep asking for interesting materials in my video.

Plan for this project

Stories are always a resource of lessons. Even though nearly all the stories that public to us are somehow touching, having special characters, full of up and downs, ending with unexpected result, stories should not be that way, actually. Any moment of life could be warm, touching, painful, frustrated, thrilled, heartbroken, or normal and sometimes being normal takes up a great part of our lives. But that does not mean that simple, normal lives are not worthy recording. Instead, they are full of sparks of everyday wisdom and meaningful choices in different possibilities. Your smile, hugs, frowns, greeting to each other, the people you meet, the food you eat, they really matters.

So the long-term goal in my mind is building up a story-sharing website that could allow people to share they stories, in whatever form - it could be plain text, images, videos. The website could also have Twitter channel, Youtube channel, Bilibili channel, Wechat Official Account, Quora account to share those meaningful content in different communities.

But first, what do I need to do now is to create several videos by myself to let others understand what I am doing. The demo video can be an Ad to call for more people to tell me their story. So the demo I record can be interview with passerby or my friends’ stories.

If everything goes well, I will complete the first demo this weekend.

  • What’s like to be alive?
  • What’s your biggest fear?
  • Where do you want to travel?
  • What’s your favorite food?
  • Who’s your best friend in high school?
  • What troubles you when you cannot fall into sleep?
  • What’s in your pocket?
  • How do you define loneliness?
  • Have you ever been to a foreign country before? What happened then?
  • What do you want to say to your mom?

Rules and Assignment

Posted on 2017-09-15

title: Rules and Assignments
date: 2017-09-13 23:10:54

tags: Creative Tech

Speaking of rules, who invent rules and for what purpose? Why did people invent such (silly or smart) rules to govern, regulate, guide their life or work? Maybe that’s for good purpose. But for me, rules is not for all the abovehead endings. Instead, it is for orginazing me life.

Here are some rules that I think vital to my life.

1.If the only reason that blocks you from buying one thing is the high price, then buy it. Forget about the price.

2. Never say no to any invitation.

3. Never forget to buy a bunch of flower to cheer up your day.

4. If you feel like giving up, then give it up for a while. Your mind never cheet you.

5. Eat if your stomach wants to eat.

6. Exercise is not for fit. It’s for self-satisfacition.

7. If you want to snack but you are not hungry, then drink water.

8. Have any idea? Instead of writing it down, instigate it. DIG IT DEEPER.

9. Forget what the other people say. Even you think their opinions are vital to you.

10. If you don’t know what to do, sleep can always be a good option.

I believe everyone was born without rules. And there are different reasons for people to adopt (or create) rules and let them be part of their life. It can be personal experience, self-learning, parent teaching or learning from friends and so-on. But I think what really matters is the impact those rules have on you.

Do they make you feel better?

Do they make you life simpler?

More importantly, do you have more self-satisfaction when you try to follow those rules?

If all the answer is YEAHHHH, then they are good rules.

Now as my students, assignment has already become part of my daily routine, or my rules FINISH ALL YOUR ASSIGNMENTS OR YOU SHALL NOT PASS(joking)

But assignments is not a drill and kill for me. For most theoretical and pedagogical classes, finishing assignment is a better way to reflect and response than just sitting in the classroom and acception what the lecturer want to make sense.

One of the best assignment, or class I have to say is the individual learning. Through all the self-learning process, I can control the timing and progress of different learning sections and have more freedom to choose what I want to learn and present in the class. I think that is what a ideal assignment should be like.

But for many of my friends, assignment can always be negative for the heavy wordload can davastate a whole lovely, sunny weekend and they are, inevitale.

So is there a proper solution for assignment to delightable?

Besides self-control learning process, there is another solution that assignment can be more interactive. Nothing like drill and kill, but like finishing one task with a clear goal and the thorough guide of teachers. More sense of achievement students get, more passion they would put into next assignment and may learn more than they thought before.
