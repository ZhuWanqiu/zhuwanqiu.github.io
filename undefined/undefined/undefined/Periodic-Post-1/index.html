Periodic Update #1

Research

Like what I posted last week, I am still trying to find as much inspirations or similar platforms as possible.

CNN iReport - I am not sure you guys heard about iReport, but I am pretty sure you heard of CNN - are running a successful(I think at least) website that allows people to share their stories, or simply post what’s going on there. If it is interested enough, CNN will contact that recorder and report that story in interview.

http://www.cnn.com/specials/opinions/cnnireport

GoodTherapy.org is a pretty warm story sharing website that collects touching stories of mental or psycho-illness and how they fight against them or be friends with them.

https://www.goodtherapy.org/submit-your-story.html

And also there is Youbute and Twitter channel #Shareyourstory that allow people to tag to their own life video or story.

Twitter:https://twitter.com/hashtag/shareyourstory?src=hash&lang=en
Youtube:https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=shareyourstory

First Step

My new camera has just delivered! And I am really excited the quality of video I am gonna shoot!

In this weekend, I will make a video that illustrate what I am going to do in this project and calls for more people to contact me to share their stories or special (or do not need to be special) moments in their life.

So in the weekend, I am going to collect materials in Time Square, Central park and wherever I pass by.

Plan for this project

Stories are always a resource of lessons. Even though nearly all the stories that public to us are somehow touching, having special characters, full of up and downs, ending with unexpected result, stories should not be that way, actually. Any moment of life could be warm, touching, painful, frustrated, thrilled, heartbroken, or normal and sometimes being normal takes up a great part of our lives. But that does not mean that simple, normal lives are not worthy recording. Instead, they are full of sparks of everyday wisdom and meaningful choices in different possibilities. Your smile, hugs, frowns, greeting to each other, the people you meet, the food you eat, they really matters.

So the long-term goal in my mind is building up a story-sharing website that could allow people to share they stories, in whatever form - it could be plain text, images, videos. The website could also have Twitter channel, Youtube channel, Bilibili channel, Wechat Official Account, Quora account to share those meaningful content in different communities.

But first, what do I need to do now is to create several videos by myself to let others understand what I am doing. The demo video can be an Ad to call for more people to tell me their story. So the demo I record can be interview with passerby or my friends’ stories.

If everything goes well, I will complete the first demo this weekend.

And the topic that comes up in my mind could be:

  • How do you define love?
  • What’s your birthday present this year?
  • What’s like to be alive?
  • What’s your biggest fear?
  • Where do you want to travel?
  • What’s your favorite food?
  • Who’s your best friend in high school?
  • What troubles you when you cannot fall into sleep?
  • What’s in your pocket?
  • How do you define loneliness?
  • Have you ever been to a foreign country before? What happened then?
  • What do you want to say to your mom?

Feedback

I told my idea to one of my friends in this class who came from documentry filming background. She mentioned that it is really hard to edit the materials into video if you have no topic, no story line. So I think I will follow on topic in one video for better editing and better viewing.

So let’s see what can I make this weekend. The project may have little shift from the original idea, but I hope I can still help people to reach out to share stuff.
